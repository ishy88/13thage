Fighter

"You'll have to deal with me first!"

Overview: The grit and guts it takes to be a true fighter may be as rare and magical as the brains and arcane spark it takes to be a wizard. Plenty of poor slobs all across the world get pressed into military duty, and some fighters start that way, but there's more to a true fighter than that: skill, discipline, toughness, and an uncanny ability to get in harm's way ... with a double dose of harm for the other guy.
 Fighters draw weapons for gold, for glory, for duty, and for the mere joy of unrestrained martial exercise. When you unsheathe your sword, what battle cry flies from your lips?
Play Style: A couple of class talents can make your job more complicated, but overall, playing a fighter is simple. You decide who to attack, roll your attack, and then figure out which flexible attack you want to use.
Backgrounds: Here are some backgrounds to get you started with your fighters:
 swordmaster, mercenary captain, sea raider, shieldwall spearman, explorer, bouncer, thug, city guardsman, Sea Wall veteran, former gladiator, former orc captive, bankrupt nobleman, duelist, goblin-hunter, former Imperial legionnaire.

Class Features

Fighters have two class features: Extra Tough and Threatening.

Fighters also have flexible attacks called maneuvers; you roll your attack and then choose which maneuver you want the attack to use. You only get to use one maneuver with each attack, so it's usually best to choose maneuvers with a few different triggering rolls.

Extra Tough

You start with nine recoveries instead of the usual eight.

Adventurer Feat: Increase your total number of recoveries by one.

Threatening

Whenever an enemy attempts to disengage from you, it takes a -4 penalty to its check. This penalty doesn't apply if you are stunned, grabbed, or otherwise incapable of making an opportunity attack.

Adventurer Feat: Whenever an enemy fails to disengage from you, you also deal 4 damage to that enemy. (At 5th level, 8 damage. At 8th level, 12 damage.)

Champion Feat: Whenever a non-mook enemy fails to disengage from you, it's vulnerable to your attacks for the rest of the battle.

Class Talents

Choose three of the following class talents. You get an additional fighter class talent at 6th level.

Cleave

Once per round, make a fighter melee attack as a free action after one of your melee attacks drops a non-mook enemy to 0 hp.

Adventurer Feat: If you have your move action available, you can use it before making your Cleave attack to reach an enemy you are not already engaged with.

Champion Feat: If you have your move action available and are already engaged, you may pop free from one enemy to reach an enemy you are not already engaged with.

Epic Feat: You gain a +4 attack bonus with your Cleave attacks.

Combat Tactics

You may use flexible attacks when making an opportunity attack. Once per round when the escalation die is even, you may subtract one from your natural attack roll.

Adventurer Feat: Once per battle, you may apply two different maneuvers to a flexible attack, so long as you meet their requirements.

Champion Feat: When you intercept an enemy, you may make an opportunity attack at a -4 penalty.

Epic Feat: Enemies you hit with opportunity attacks are vulnerable until the end of your next turn.

Comeback Strike

Once per battle as a free action, make another attack with a –2 penalty after your first fighter attack during your turn misses.

Adventurer Feat: You no longer take the -2 penalty to your Comeback Strike attacks. In addition, if your Comeback Strike attack critically hits, you do not expend its use.

Champion Feat: When the escalation die reaches 3, you gain one additional use of Comeback Strike. In addition, when recharging a magic armor power, roll twice and take the highest result.

Epic Feat: You gain a +4 attack bonus with your Comeback Strike attacks.

Counter-Attack

Once per round when an enemy misses you with a natural odd melee attack roll, you can make a basic melee attack dealing half damage against that enemy as a free action. (The attack can't use any limited abilities or flexible attack maneuvers.)

Adventurer Feat: Your Counter-Attack attack now deals full damage.

Champion Feat: When the escalation die is even, you can use Counter-Attack once per turn instead of once per round.

Epic Feat: Once per battle, counter attack may trigger on a natural even miss on a melee attack roll. Deadeye Archer

Your attacks with d8 ranged weapons (heavy crossbow, longbow) now deal d10 damage per level. Your attacks with d6 ranged weapons (light crossbow, shortbow) now deal d8 damage per level. In addition, your misses with basic ranged attacks deal damage equal to your level.

Adventurer Feat: If you spend a quick action to aim before making a ranged basic attack, add a +5 bonus to the damage if you miss.

Champion Feat: Once per battle, expand your crit range with a fighter ranged attack by 4 (usually to 16+) for that attack.

Epic Feat: Your crit range with ranged weapon attacks expands by 1 (usually to 19+).

Heavy Warrior

Once per battle, when you are hit by an attack that targets AC, as a free action, you can take half damage from that attack instead.

Adventurer Feat: When the escalation die reaches 3, you gain an additional use of Heavy Warrior. You can also use this talent against an attack that targets PD.

Champion Feat: Whenever you use Heavy Warrior, you may immediately roll to recharge your magic armor's power (if any). Add the escalation die to this roll.

Epic Feat: You can also use the power against an attack that targets MD.

Power Attack

Once per battle, when you hit with an attack, you may deal extra damage to the target of that attack equal to 1d4 damage per level if you are using a one-handed weapon, or 1d6 damage per level with a two-handed weapon.

Adventurer Feat: You may also choose to deal the additional Power Attack damage on a missed attack. When you deal damage to an enemy with Power Attack, you may choose to pop it free from all creatures.

Champion Feat: When the escalation die reaches 3, you gain one additional use of Power Attack.

Epic Feat: One-handed weapon damage using Power Attack increases to 1d6 per level; two-handed weapon damage using Power Attack increases to 1d8 per level.

Preparation

You can generate the effect of any maneuver that you know which requires a missed attack roll as a standard action instead of making a flexible melee attack to see which maneuver you are able to trigger.

Adventurer Feat: Once per battle, if the escalation die is 3 or higher, you may use Preparation as a move action.

Champion Feat: Once per battle, if the escalation die is 5 or higher, you may use Preparation as a quick action.

Skilled Intercept

Once per round as a free action, roll a save (11+) to make an enemy moving to attack one of your allies attack you instead. Take half damage from this attack if you are wearing heavy armor.

Adventurer Feat:: You gain a bonus to your Skilled Intercept save equal to the escalation die.

Champion Feat: Once per battle, you can take no damage from the moving enemy's attack.

Epic Feat: You gain a +4 bonus to your Skilled Intercept save.

Tough as Iron

Once per battle, you can rally using a quick action instead of a standard action.

Adventurer Feat: Once per day, you can rally twice during a battle as a quick action, without needing to roll a save for the second rally.

Champion Feat: Increase your total number of recoveries by 2.

Epic Feat: When you roll a natural 18+ with an attack, you gain an additional use of Tough As Iron this battle.

1st Level Manuevers

Bash
Flexible melee attack
Triggering Roll: Any natural odd roll
Special: If you are wielding a shield, Bash also triggers on any hit
Effect: The target pops free of yourself or one ally of your choice. Once per battle, you can also daze the target until the end of its next turn.

Adventurer Feat: The target instead pops free of you and any number of allies

Champion Feat: You can choose to daze an enemy twice per battle. In addition, the daze effect is now save ends.

Epic Feat: The target is also knocked prone.

Brace For It
Flexible melee attack
Trigerering Roll: Any natural odd roll.
Effect: Until the start of your next turn, critical hits against you become normal hits.

Adventurer Feat: Until the start of your next turn, the first nearby ally that would receive a critical hit instead receives a normal hit.

Carve an Opening
Flexible melee or ranged attack
Triggering Roll: Any natural odd roll
Effect: Your crit range with melee and ranged attacks expands by a cumulative +1 this battle until you score a melee or ranged critical hit. When you score a melee or ranged critical hit, your crit range drops back to normal.

Champion Feat: The crit range bonus from carve an opening is +2 instead of +1.

Deadly Assault
Flexible melee or ranged attack
Triggering Roll: Any natural even hit
Effect: Deal extra damage equal to 2 x your level with this attack. (At 8th level, 3 x level)

Adventurer Feat: Deadly Assault now also triggers on a natural 16+

Defensive Fighting
Flexible melee attack
Triggering Roll: Any natural odd roll
Special: If you are wielding a shield, Defensive Fighting also triggers on a natural 16+
Effect: Gain a +2 bonus to AC until the end of your next turn. (This bonus does not stack with itself)

Champion Feat: You also gain the bonus to Physical Defense.

Epic Feat: You also gain the bonus to Mental Defense.

Grim Intent
Flexible melee attack
Triggering Roll: Any natural even roll
Effect: The next time you would deal miss damage with a melee attack, add a WEAPON die to that damage. (At 5th level, 2 WEAPON dice. At 8th level, 3 WEAPON dice)

Heavy Blows
Flexible melee attack
Triggering Roll: Any natural even hit
Effect: The target is knocked prone.

Adventurer Feat: Heavy Blows also triggers on a natural even miss.

Keep Up The Pressure
Flexible melee or ranged attack
Triggering Roll: Any natural odd roll
Special: If you are wielding two weapons, Keep Up The Pressure also triggers on any miss
Effect: Gain a +2 bonus to attack against the target for the rest of the battle. (This bonus does not stack with itself)

Champion Feat: The first time you attack the target after using this manuever the bonus is +4 instead.

Second Shot
Flexible ranged attack
Special: You can only use this manuever only once per round.
Triggering Roll: Any natural even roll or a natural 16+
Effect: After this attack, you can make a basic ranged attack with the same weapon with a –4 attack penalty. If the triggering roll was less than a natural 16, you can't use any maneuvers with the second attack and the attack deals half damage.

Adventurer Feat: The attack penalty decreases to –2.

Champion Feat: Second shot no longer deals half damage if triggered with a natural even roll.

Taunt
Flexible melee attack
Triggering Roll: Any natural odd roll
Effect: The target cannot attack your allies on its next turn

Champion Feat: This is now a save ends effect.

3rd Level Manuevers

Hack and Slash
Flexible melee attack
Special: You can use this maneuver only once per round.
Triggering Roll: Any natural even roll
Special: If you are wielding two weapons, Hack and Slash also triggers on a natural 16+
Effect: Make another melee weapon attack against a different, engaged target at a -4 penalty. You can't use maneuvers with this second attack.

Adventurer Feat: The attack penalty decreases to -2.

Champion Feat: If there is no foe engaged with you that is a valid target for your second Hack and Slash attack, as a free action you can move to a nearby foe before making the attack.

Make 'em Flinch
Flexible ranged attack
Triggering Roll: Any natural even roll
Effect: The next attack you or your allies make against the target has a +4 attack bonus

Punish Them
Flexible melee attack
Special: You can use this maneuver only when you make an opportunity attack.
Triggering Roll: Any natural even roll
Effect: The target is dazed until the end of its turn. If the target was moving, it stops moving and loses the rest of its move action.

Adventurer Feat: Once per battle, this manuever can be used on a flexible attack you make on your turn.

Champion Feat: The daze effect is now save ends.

Epic Feat: The target is now weakened (save ends) instead of dazed.

Steady Now
Flexible melee attack
Special: You can only use this maneuver once per battle
Triggering Roll: Any natural odd roll
Effect: You gain temporary hit points equal to 5 x your level.

Champion Feat: The temporary hit points increase to 10 x your level.

Strong Guard
Flexible melee attack
Triggering Roll: Any natural odd roll
Special: If you are wielding a shield, Strong Guard also triggers on any miss.
Effect: One ally you can touch (including an ally engaged with the same enemy as you) gains a +2 AC bonus until you can no longer touch them.

Champion Feat: The bonus also applies to PD.

Epic Feat: The bonus increases to +3.

5th Level Manuevers

A Dozen Cuts
Flexible melee attack
Triggering Roll: Any natural even hit
Effect: The target takes 10 ongoing damage. (15 at 8th level)
Special: If you are wielding two weapons, this ongoing damage requires a hard (16+) save to overcome

Champion Feat: Once per battle, you can trigger a dozen cuts with a natural odd hit.

Arrow To The Knee
Flexible ranged attack
Triggering Roll: Any natural even hit
Effect: The target is stuck until the end of its next turn.

Champion Feat: This effect is now save ends.

Epic Feat: The effect now lasts until the end of battle.

Hero's Skill
Flexible melee or ranged attack
Triggering Roll: Any natural even miss
Effect: Add +2 to the attack roll. The attack deals half damage on a hit.

Champion Feat: Add +4 to the attack roll instead of +2.

Epic Feat: The damage is no longer halved on a hit.

Sword Master's Anticipation
Flexible melee attack
Special: You must have the Skilled Intercept talent to use this maneuver.
Triggering Roll: Any natural odd roll
Effect: The next time you use Skilled Intercept this battle, your Skilled Intercept save automatically succeeds.

Champion Feat: You may also make an opportunity attack against the target of your automatic skilled intercept.

Epic Feat: Once per battle, you may use Sword Master's Anticipation and another maneuver on the same flexible attack.

Spinning Charge

Triggering Roll: Any natural even hit
Effect: You can pop free from the target, move to a different nearby enemy, and make a basic melee attack against that enemy. You can't use any maneuvers with the second attack.

Epic Feat: Once per battle, you can make a flexible attack instead of a basic attack with this manuever

Sword of Destiny

Triggering Roll: Any natural odd roll or a Natural 20
Special: You can only trigger this manuever off of a natural odd roll once per battle

1st Level Tactics

Basic Tactical Strike
Quick action
Recharge 11+ after battle
Target: One nearby ally
Effect: The target can make a basic attack as a free action.

Adventurer Feat: On a hit, the attack gains a +5 damage bonus (+10 at 5th level; +15 at 8th level).

Champion Feat: The target gains an +4 attack bonus with that attack.

Epic Feat: The recharge roll is now 6+.

Enforce Clarity
Quick action
Recharge 16+ after battle
Target: One nearby ally
Effect: One non-last gasp effect on the target ends (including effects that don't require a save).

Adventurer Feat: The recharge roll is now 11+.

Champion Feat: You can now target one additional nearby ally with this tactic.

Epic Feat: The recharge roll is now 6+.

Just Stay Calm
Quick action
Recharge 16+ after battle
Special: You can only use this tactic when the escalation die is 2+
Effect: Decrease the escalation die by 1. Then 1d3 of your nearby allies can heal using a recovery.

Adventurer Feat: The tactic now affects 1d3 + 1 nearby allies.

Champion Feat: You can include yourself as one of the targets.

Epic Feat: When you use this tactic, you can spend an additional command point to avoid decreasing the escalation die.

Outmaneuver
Quick action
Close-quarters attack
At-Will
Special: You can only use this tactic when you have 0 command points, and only once per round.
Target: The nearby enemy with the highest Mental Defense
Attack: 4 + Level vs. MD
Hit: You gain 1 command point.

Adventurer Feat: When you are engaged with one or more enemies, you can target the enemy with the highest MD you are engaged with instead of the nearest enemy.

Champion Feat: When you attack with this tactic and roll a natural even hit, you gain 2 command points instead of 1.

Epic Feat: Once per battle as a free action when you hit with this tactic, the target also takes 1d10 psychic damage for each point on the escalation die.

3rd Level Tactics

Finish This!
Quick action
Recharge 16+ after battle
Special: You can only use this tactic when one enemy is left in the battle and the escalation die is 4+.
Effect: You can spend between 1 and 3 command points. The crit range of your allies' attacks against the remaining enemy expands by the number of command points you spent. This effect lasts until the end of the battle.

Adventurer Feat: You can now use this tactic when the escalation die is 3+.

Champion Feat: You can now use this tactic when one or two enemies are left in the battle. The effect applies to both enemies.

Epic Feat: You can now spend between 1 and 5 command points on the effect.

Scramble
Quick action
Recharge 16+ after battle
Targets: You and 1d3 nearby allies
Effect: Each target can take a move action as a free action, starting with you and proceeding in the order of your choice.

Adventurer Feat: Disengage checks made using this free action gain a +5 bonus.

Champion Feat: The recharge roll is now 11+.

Epic Feat: Each target can also make a basic attack as a free action after taking the move action.

Swordwork
Free action
Recharge 16+ after battle
Special: You can only use this tactic when the escalation die is 4+
Effect: You can make a basic melee attack as a quick action once each turn until the end of the battle. You can't gain command points from your Fight from the Front class feature using the extra attacks granted by this tactic.

Adventurer Feat: You can now use this tactic when the escalation die is 3+.

Champion Feat: You can now gain command points from your Fight from the Front class feature using swordwork attacks.

Epic Feat: The recharge roll is now 11+.

5th Level Tactics

Advanced Tactical Strike
Quick action
Recharge 16+ after battle
Target: One nearby ally
Effect: The target can make an at-will attack as a free action.

Champion Feat: The first time you use this tactic each battle, make a recharge roll for it at the start of your next turn, adding the escalation die to the roll. The recharge roll after the battle, if any, doesn't gain a bonus from the escalation die.

Epic Feat: The recharge roll is now 11+.

Buck Up!
Quick action
Recharge 16+ after battle
Targets: You and 1d4 nearby allies
Effect: Each target gains temporary hit points equal to the number of hit points it gains when it heals using a recovery.

Champion Feat: Add 10 to the temporary hit points each target gains (15 at 8th level).

Epic Feat: One of the targets can also heal using a recovery.

7th Level Tactics

Climactic Battle
Quick action
Recharge 16+ after battle
Effect: Until the end of the battle, the escalation die becomes a d8 instead of a d6. Then roll a d20. If you roll 11+, increase the escalation die by 1.

Champion Feat: If the d20 roll is 16+, increase the escalation die by 2 instead of 1.

Epic Feat: When the escalation die reaches 8, you gain 1d6 command points and can make recharge rolls for all your tactics.

On Your Feet, Maggots!
Quick action
Recharge 16+ after battle

Effect: The targets can each heal using two recoveries but is dazed until the end of its next turn.

Champion Feat: If the escalation die is 3+, there is no dazed effect.

Epic Feat: This tactic now targets up to 1d4 + 1 allies.

Saving Will
Quick action
Recharge 16+ after battle
Target: One nearby ally
Effect: The target gains a +5 bonus to all saves until the end of your next turn.

Champion Feat: This tactic can now be used as a free action.

Epic Feat: You can now target a far away ally.

Supreme Tactical Strike
